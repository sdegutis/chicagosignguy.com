I did this survey for a few hours,
until some group of hooligans came up
and wrote all over the No section.
So I just dropped the survey on the ground,
walked about 30 feet away slowly,
and sat down feeling completely defeated.
Even before that, I already felt so defeated all day.
But that was the nail in the coffin.

I know nobody reads this blog,
so I get to give you the inside scoop,
"you" being nobody,
since nobody will read this article.

I'm flat broke. I have a little more than enough for a gym membership for the month so I can shower.
I've been living off a little less than $200 per month in book royalties for a few months now.
I was sleeping in my car all winter, even when it was -30 F outside, taking the Metra to Chicago.
Last week I sold my car and moved here, and now I sleep on the ground outside in the middle of the loop.
It was 36 F outside the first week I slept out here, and I had on only what I typically wear. But I lived.
You'll probably see me. And probably so will people with no morals, eventually.
Everything I carry with me is everything I own now. I expect it will gradually be lost to robbery.

My life is basically over.
I have no right to expect it to improve.
I've hit rock bottom, and rightfully so.

But throughout the winter,
as my royalty checks kept decreasing each month,
and as I needed money for gas and food and car insurance,
I decided to do something that has never come naturally to me:
have *hope*.

Which is when the idea came to me
to sell Terrible Advice for the low price of $3.
And the same hope inspired the idea for
selling Awkward Smalltalk for $2,
and Premium Snowballs for $1.
When I had hope, I was able to get creative.

I do not believe a man
who is capable of working
should beg or accept handouts.
I will dig myself out of this hole or die trying.

But it's not even a hole, per se.
It's a *choice*.
Some relatives have offered me a place to stay.
But no, that's not right.
A capable man should provide for himself.

Yes, I dug myself into this hole
through my seemingly irreparable incompetence.
And I deserve to be here.
And I deserve to die here.
And I will probably die here.
But I will dig myself out or die trying.

My overarching goal in life is to become a *good man*.
I have several smaller goals.
I want to make friends.
I want to fall in love.
I want to be financially stable.
I want to cultivate and use my talents.
I want to pay off my debts (mostly overdue taxes).
I want to stop sleeping on the ground at night.
Especially since tonight it's going to rain.
But tonight I do not have a choice.
So I will become a good man, or die trying.
Either is fine with me.

Last autumn, I had no money and no prospects of money.
For most of 2025, I wrote software, day and night, as much as I could,
hoping that I could sell it somehow and use that for a living.
It did not happen. I wasted almost a whole year writing useless software.

I wondered what God wanted to use me for.
My life seems like a useless waste.
And then Charlie Kirk died.
Me being Catholic, I knew how wrong his religious take was.
So I took all his political takes with a large dose of salt.
But it looked like such an *easy* way out of life, to debate publicly.
So I held up a sign saying "abortion is murder, prove me wrong,"
thinking that maybe someone would kill me too.
But it didn't happen.
And I just pissed people off.
And I only had one debate with a few people who I don't recall their faces.
And it was a pointless debate.
Like most of them are.
So I gave that up after two Sundays of trying it.

A few weeks later, or maybe the next week, I don't remember,
I just felt so alone and like a pointless existence.
And for some reason, I had the idea to make a sign.
I think the first one was on the back of a sign
that someone made for a sports game or political rally.
The back was blank, and they threw it in the trash after folding it up.

Here's that sign.

![How along do you feel](/img/howalonedoyoufeel.jpg)

All the change on the table is all the money I had left to my name.
It wasn't even enough for a train ticket back.
I had to wait until my next royalty check to come back to Chicago.

People liked the sign, so I did more.
I felt useful for the first time in a long time, maybe ever.
So I kept doing more.
And I really needed that interaction with other people, individual people.
Whether it was just a simple tally, or a conversation, or a thanks.
It all helped me feel useful.

Eventually I realized I could tie this into my book.
I was trying to write a theology book for most of 2024,
about love,
about Adam and Eve,
about Mary and Joseph,
and Jesus and Mary,
and about Jesus and the Church,
and about fairy tales and dragons and knights in shining armor,
and about how they all tie together.
Because they do.

So I started telling people I was doing the surveys for my book.
And in all honesty,
that book is probably the only way I can make enough money to afford a place to live at this point.
I have 15 years of professional software experience that is now useless thanks to AI.
I have 15 years of studying theology in my personal time, which has gone to waste.
I have a few months of drawing experience,
and I can't tell whether it's a natural talent or not,
because people have this *unfortunate* habit of praising people's work regardless of talent,
especially when you don't actually *have* talent (to encourage you I suppose, and also avoid discouraging you),
which makes it *incredibly hard* to know if you actually have talent or not.
And I have the ability to learn piano pieces *very* quickly, which I have no idea how to utilize.
And I have experience making signs, which is rather pointless though.
And I have an unusual but fun sense of humor that came up with signs that made tons of people laugh.

And, of course, I have a constant feeling of hopelessness and despair,
perpetually convinced that my life is over and my world is ending and nothing matters and everything is pointless.
But that train of thought leads nowhere.

So I'm going to keep going to the gym every day and working out really hard and getting stronger.
And I'm going to keep abusing the Taco Bell app to get free food until it stops me.
And I'm going to keep practicing piano at the library every single day of the week.
And I'm going to keep hoping, despite all odds, that things will improve, as long as I keep persevering and trying to become a *good man*.

I'm tired of being a loser.
I'm tired of feeling like a waste of human existence.
I'm tired of being useless to everyone.
So I will find a way to be useful to society, or die trying.

I will not accept handouts.
So don't even think to offer anything.
And I don't want or need sympathy.
I'm tired of feeling like a loser.
Men used to build houses and fight off wolves with their bare hands.
I'm going to become a good man, or die trying.

Anyone who befriends me *now*,
while I'm in this very deep hole,
and when I have nothing to offer you but my friendship,
these are the only people whose friendship I can trust.
Because I know for a fact that they like me for *me*,
and not for my money or fame or influence or anything else.

Because I'm either going to get out of this hole with surprising underdog success, or die trying.
And in either case, it will be a success story,
because I'm not giving into either loss of hope or loss of integrity,
and I'm still following my principles to the end.

I don't mind being broke and sleeping outside.
It's basically like playing Minecraft, but in real life.
What I do mind is living an empty, lonely, meaningless life.
I'm tired of being a loser with no friends or helpful career or love interest.
So I'm going to make a real difference in the world somehow, or die trying.

So if you see me sitting outside selling graphite or charcoal sketches, that's me working my way up.
And if you see me sitting outside busking with a broken guitar, I must have bought it from a thrift store with my sketch profits.
And if you see me playing a cheap piano in that spot, I probably bought it with my guitar busking money.
And if you see me selling copies of my new book on the corner, it's because I finally finished it.

Although, I do think I need a muse.
I think I need to fall in love as the last step of inspiration for that book.
I wonder if I'll find someone who could love me despite this horrifying situation I'm in.
I wonder if I'll meet someone who can look past that and see into my soul and love me for me.
Granted, nobody has known about this situation for the past two weeks that I've been sleeping on the street.
So I think it should be fine. I think I have a chance.
